# The Brainstorming Vault: High-Value Discovery Methods

> **Expert's Note:** This vault contains a curated collection of brainstorming techniques categorized by their cognitive "vibe." These methods are the tools we use in the **High-Fidelity Discovery Track** to ensure that our "Product Constitution" is built on diverse, stress-tested, and creative logic.

---

## ü§ù Collaborative Methods
*Focus: Building momentum and ensuring every voice contributes to the "Logic-Touch" process.*

### Yes And Building
*   **WHAT:** A technique where each participant builds on the previous idea using "Yes, and..."
*   **WHY:** To prevent "Idea Killing" early in the brainstorming phase and build massive creative momentum.
*   **HOW:** Start with a seed idea. Every follow-up must begin with "Yes, and we could also..."
*   **WHEN:** During initial ideation sessions where energy is low or people are being too critical.

### Brain Writing Round Robin
*   **WHAT:** Silent, written idea generation followed by passing the paper to the next person.
*   **WHY:** Gives quieter voices equal contribution and prevents "Groupthink."
*   **HOW:** Write 3 ideas on a paper. Pass it. The next person adds 3 more building on those.
*   **WHEN:** When dealing with high-ego teams or when you need a high volume of diverse ideas quickly.

## üé® Creative & Divergent Methods
*Focus: Breaking through mental blocks and finding "The Founder's Secret."*

### First Principles Thinking
*   **WHAT:** Stripping away all assumptions to rebuild from fundamental truths.
*   **WHY:** Essential for breakthrough innovation where the "Standard Way" is inefficient.
*   **HOW:** Ask "What do we know for certain is true?" Rebuild the solution from those bedrock facts.
*   **WHEN:** When a project feels "stuck" in a traditional architectural pattern that isn't working.

### Reversal Inversion
*   **WHAT:** Deliberately flipping the problem upside down.
*   **WHY:** Reveals hidden assumptions and fresh angles by looking at the "Worst Case" or the "Opposite."
*   **HOW:** Ask "How could we make this problem worse?" and then invert those answers into solutions.
*   **WHEN:** When conventional approaches have failed and you need a radical new perspective.

## üîç Deep & Analytical Methods
*Focus: Root causes and system integrity (The "Logic-Touch" core).*

### Five Whys
*   **WHAT:** Drilling down through layers of causation.
*   **WHY:** To solve problems at their source rather than just treating symptoms.
*   **HOW:** Ask "Why?" to every answer until you reach the fundamental driver (usually 5 layers deep).
*   **WHEN:** During **Pre-Mortems** or when diagnosing a recurring system failure.

### Assumption Reversal
*   **WHAT:** Identifying and flipping core project assumptions.
*   **WHY:** Necessary for "Paradigm Shifts" in product strategy.
*   **HOW:** List every assumption you are making about the user/market. Flip them. Ask "What if the opposite were true?"
*   **WHEN:** During the transition from **Vision to Strategy**.

## üé≠ Theatrical & Perspective Methods
*Focus: Empathy and external viewpoints.*

### Alien Anthropologist
*   **WHAT:** Examining familiar problems through "foreign" or "outsider" eyes.
*   **WHY:** Reveals hidden cultural or technical assumptions we take for granted.
*   **HOW:** Adopt the persona of an outsider who knows nothing of your industry. Ask "Why is this done this way?"
*   **WHEN:** To simplify a complex UI or onboarding flow that has become too "insider-heavy."

### Persona Journey
*   **WHAT:** Embodying different archetypes to access diverse wisdom.
*   **WHY:** Taps into specific user needs that the internal team might overlook.
*   **HOW:** Assign a "Persona" to each brainstormer. They must only advocate for that character's needs.
*   **WHEN:** When the "No-Code" group is refining the **User Personas** in the PRD.

## üåÄ Wild & Anti-Fragile Methods
*Focus: Stress-testing and maverick thinking.*

### Chaos Engineering (Brainstorming Edition)
*   **WHAT:** Deliberately "breaking" the proposed logic to find robust solutions.
*   **WHY:** Builds "Anti-fragility" into the Product Constitution.
*   **HOW:** Ask "What if the database disappears?" or "What if the CEO changes their mind?" Build the solution to survive these shocks.
*   **WHEN:** During the **Technical Design (TDD)** phase.

### Drunk History Retelling
*   **WHAT:** Explaining complex ideas with uninhibited simplicity.
*   **WHY:** Removes overthinking barriers to find the "Raw Truth" of the value proposition.
*   **HOW:** Explain the product logic as if you were in a relaxed, casual setting with no jargon allowed.
*   **WHEN:** When the PRD has become too bloated with corporate-speak or technical jargon.

## üåø Biomimetic & Quantum Methods
*Focus: Using natural and physical laws as metaphors for software.*

### Nature's Solutions (Biomimicry)
*   **WHAT:** Studying how nature solves similar problems (e.g., ant colonies, ecosystems).
*   **WHY:** Taps into 3.8 billion years of "evolutionary logic."
*   **HOW:** Identify a biological system that mirrors your software problem (e.g., "Our notification system is like an ant's pheromone trail").
*   **WHEN:** When designing **Distributed Systems** or **Complex Workflows**.

### Superposition Collapse
*   **WHAT:** Holding multiple contradictory solutions simultaneously until constraints force a single outcome.
*   **WHY:** Prevents premature "Optimization" that closes off better pathways.
*   **HOW:** Refuse to pick a single path. Document 3 valid but conflicting paths in the PRD until technical reality "collapses" them into one.
*   **WHEN:** During the **High-Fidelity Discovery Track** when the path forward is ambiguous.
